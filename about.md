# 🏛️ About The Divan and the Sword (TDS)

**The Divan and the Sword (TDS)** is an independent scholarly research project dedicated to the study of Iranian political tradition, bureaucracy, governance, and the Persianate world. The project adopts a transparent, evidence-based, and fully documented approach to the historical analysis of administrative institutions, political legitimacy, and institutional continuity in the Iranian tradition.

---

## 🎯 Mission

To advance the understanding of Iranian political thought and practice through rigorous, open, and interdisciplinary research, focusing on the historical interplay between administrative systems (the Divan) and political/military authority (the Sword).

---

## 👁️ Vision

To establish a sustainable, open-access scholarly platform that serves as a reference for researchers, students, and enthusiasts of Iranian and Persianate studies, fostering collaboration across disciplinary and geographical boundaries.

---

## ⚖️ Core Principles

The project operates under a strict set of governance principles that ensure transparency, traceability, and scholarly integrity:

- **Rule Zero** – No statement without evidence.
- **Evidence Before Status** – Status changes only after evidence is provided.
- **Repository First** – The repository is the single source of truth.
- **Atomic Commits** – Each change in a single, documented commit.
- **Traceability** – All steps documented with clear commits.
- **Version Stability** – Released versions are immutable.

---

## 🌍 Open Scholarship

TDS is committed to transparent research, open documentation, and long-term accessibility. Whenever possible, project materials, documentation, and research outputs are made publicly available to support scholarly collaboration and reproducibility.

---

## 📚 Research Areas

The project focuses on the following research areas:

- **Iranian Political Tradition** – Concepts of kingship, justice, and legitimacy.
- **Bureaucracy and Administration** – The evolution of the Divan system and its institutions.
- **Governance and Statecraft** – Theories and practices of governance in Persianate societies.
- **Persianate World** – Cultural and intellectual networks across the Persian-speaking world.
- **Historiography** – Critical analysis of historical sources and narratives.
